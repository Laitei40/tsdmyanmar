Who We Are

The people and story behind TSD Myanmar.

Who we are

Together for Sustainable Development (TSD) is a registered Non-Government Organization in Myanmar, based in Lailenpi Town in the Mara region of Southern Chin State, where it serves Mara communities and beyond. TSD's work focuses on promoting sustainable development in rural communities through four main programme areas: Education, Food Security, Inclusion, and Disaster Risk Reduction & Relief. We have a particular focus on sustainable education, and view education as part of all our work, as we empower people with knowledge, skills, and the tools they need to play an active part in building more resilient, inclusive, and delightful communities. Read more about what we do.

Our team

The staff of TSD are all residents from communities throughout the Mara Region, with an aim to have a good gender balance and include people with disabilities. The team is driven by its faith-based foundation, a strong sense of self-reliance, and a belief in the difference that can be made when people come together with purpose. TSD has invested in the capacity of its staff, building on their local knowledge, community mobilisation skills, and passion to serve the most vulnerable.

Our core values

Love & Sharing
"Love your neighbour as yourself" is the golden rule for TSD as a Christian faith-based organisation. TSD is inspired to turn this rule into concrete action by sharing love through our work.

Equality & Justice
We believe that everyone has the same creator and has equal rights. We aim to see social justice embodied through our work.

Accountability & Transparency
Those we serve are our true bosses in this work. We aim to keep our commitments to them as well as our donors, and we are eager to learn from our mistakes.

Our story

TSD was born out of the community's recognition of its own needs during a time of great hardship for the Mara ethnic minority population of southern Chin State. This remote, mountainous region has long struggled with geographic isolation, lack of infrastructure, poor access to services, and food insecurity. Read more about where we work.

In 2003, Mai Ki, the region's first woman minister, felt a calling to lead her community "to be Blessing for Others" by creating education opportunities for children, joining the Service & Development Department of the Mara Evangelical Church.

Mai Ki, together with a small, hardworking team of staff — largely working voluntarily in the beginning — mobilised community members to work together towards positive change. Early activities ranged from constructing rural roads by hand to installing community rice mills and starting women's self-help groups, at a time when this region of Myanmar was completely cut off from international visits or communication. This gave rise to a strong sense of self-sufficiency and a commitment to community ownership and sustainability that continues today.

As of 2017, TSD became an independent NGO, separate from the church. The organisation has grown in professionalism and scope, while holding on to its original passion and strong community roots. Togetherness has remained a key theme throughout TSD's journey — the work today is only possible through the support of communities, the church, close collaboration with community-based organisations and other local actors, and support from international partners.

Our partners

TSD works alongside local partners including the Mara Self-Help Group Federation, Mara Evangelical Church, Health and Hope Myanmar, Mara Students Association, Mara Chano Py (Mara Women Organization), Mara Thyutlia Py (Mara Youth Organization), and Lisu Theological Seminary, together with international partners Bread for the World (Germany), Hildersheim Blind Mission (Germany), Diakonie Katastrophehilfe (Germany), and Mission East (Denmark).
