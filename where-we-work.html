Where We Work

The regions and communities we partner with.

Southern Chin State

TSD is based in a remote area of Chin State, Myanmar (Burma), in the region inhabited by the Mara population — one of Myanmar's many ethnic minorities. The organisation has past or current projects in many of the 53 Mara communities in this area, and plans to expand to serve other parts of southern Chin State.

Chin State is a beautiful mountainous region with natural resources including forest products, waterways, and minerals — but the terrain poses a challenge for farming, makes road construction difficult, and brings the frequent threat of landslides. With few roads, much transport in southern Chin State takes place by motorbike on narrow mountain paths, for those who can afford it; others walk for days on foot to reach healthcare, markets, or school examinations.

The state's sparse population of approximately 479,000 people mostly live in small rural villages, largely isolated from the rest of Myanmar. People in southern Chin State are almost exclusively subsistence farmers, and the church plays a significant social role in Chin society, contributing to a strong sense of social cohesion and resilience.

Why we work here

Chin State is one of the least developed states in Myanmar, with the highest poverty rates and lowest GDP per capita in the country — 58% of the population lives below the poverty line (UNDP, World Bank & Myanmar Central Statistical Organization). Food insecurity is a chronic problem, with the highest rates of child malnutrition in the country: 40.3% of children affected by stunting (Ministry of Health & Sports). Education outcomes are also poor — many children drop out early, and Chin State often records the highest failure rate for the country's final matriculation exams, with over 80% of students typically failing. TSD's programmes respond directly to these challenges, working alongside communities on education, food security, inclusion, and disaster risk reduction & relief.
