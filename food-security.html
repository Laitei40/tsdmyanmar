Our approach in practice

TSD trains farmers in organic upland agricultural methods — including terracing, composting, pest control, seed-saving, and crop management — and provides the tools, seeds, and materials needed to kick-start new farming approaches. Staff walk alongside pioneering farmers, connect them with experts, and trial new methods on a model farm before sharing them more widely. Training also covers nutrition, food preparation and preservation, construction of gravity-fed water systems for irrigation, marketing skills, and household financial management.

Farmers are supported to form cooperatives and to run Community Daily Needs Shops, allowing households to sell surplus produce jointly and purchase goods together at better prices. So far, 209 farmer families are pioneering these new methods, and 100 acres have been converted from shifting cultivation to permanent, sustainable farms, with 4 communities becoming models for sustainable water, forest, and land management.
